AI-native talent is grown, not found.

Why every Vietnamese teammate at xR&D came through the same partner-university pipeline — and why we don't recruit at scale, ever.

Nghia Founding Partner · Talent
22 Apr 2026
Issue 007

There is a question I get asked at every founder dinner in Tokyo: "where do you find your engineers?" The honest answer is uncomfortable for the people asking. We don't find them. We grow them, in cohorts, over years, through one partner university in Da Nang. There is no shortlist, no recruiter, no LinkedIn outreach. There is a curriculum.

01 The pipelineOne university, one curriculum.

The pipeline begins in second year of undergraduate study at our partner university. Students join an extracurricular track — Japanese language, applied AI engineering, two semesters of project work under live engagements at the studio. The cohort is small. We accept twelve a year. We graduate roughly nine of those into the company.

  • Year 1 of pipeline: Japanese-language foundation, AI tooling onboarding
  • Year 2: paired internship under senior engineers, contributing to live engagements
  • Year 3: graduation review, offer, three-month integration cohort
  • Year 4 onward: full team member, eligible for client-side placement after twelve months

02 Why grownThe shape of the engineer.

The market trains engineers to use tools. We train them to operate inside an AI-native workflow as the default. There is a difference. A bootcamp graduate uses an AI assistant when stuck. A pipeline graduate writes the prompt before they write the function — and treats the assistant as a first reviewer, not a fallback.

That difference compounds. Three years into a pipeline graduate's career, they have done thousands of AI-mediated reviews and built deep instincts about where the model helps and where it lies. A bootcamp graduate has done dozens. The gap doesn't close — it widens, because the pipeline graduate keeps practicing inside the same operational frame they trained in.

03 Why one sourceThe cohort matters more than the candidate.

We are sometimes asked why we don't hire from elsewhere. The answer is cultural rather than technical. The cohort culture — peer review, bilingual habit, AI-as-default — is the asset. A great solo hire from another company comes with their own set of habits and friction patterns. We've tried it twice. Both times the friction was higher than the value.

The first cohort wasn't ready. The second cohort was. The difference was that we'd learned how to teach the thing we were teaching.
Nghia · Founding Partner

This isn't a forever rule. As the cohorts mature, lateral hires from other AI-native companies will likely make sense. But for the first five years, the pipeline is the only intake.

04 The costYears of investment per graduate.

The pipeline is expensive in time. Each engineer represents roughly two years of unpaid teaching by senior staff before they ship in earnest. The cost is real and it doesn't get cheaper. What we get back is engineers who hold the operational standard from day one, with no retraining and no friction.

Math we tell clients: the pipeline graduate ships in a window 5–10× faster than equivalent traditional teams, with quality bars that hold up under client review. That delta pays back the upfront investment within the first twelve months of paid work, and compounds from there.

05 What we don't doRecruiting at scale.

We do not recruit at scale and we will not start. Scaling recruitment is the standard play for an IT services company; it is incompatible with the kind of company we want to be. Each new cohort is a culture decision. We can absorb roughly nine engineers a year without diluting the operating standard. More than that, we lose what makes the pipeline work.

This bounds the company size. The four-business model is calibrated to the pipeline's output rate, not to demand. We turn down engagements rather than hire to meet them.

06 What this means for clientsWhat you're buying.

When a client engages xR&D, the engineer on the other side of the call is a pipeline graduate. They've been operating inside our standard for years. They review code with the AI as a partner, write specs in three languages, and have shipped under JP-client conditions before. That's the asset, and it's the only one we offer.

07 What's nextCohort 03.

Cohort 03 starts year-two work this summer. Twelve students, currently completing their second-year university semester, ready for paired engagement work. We'll publish a follow-up piece on their year-two engagements once the first ones ship.
