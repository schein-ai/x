The first 5 engineers placed onto a JP client.

How a small AI-native team integrated with an existing IT services firm in Tokyo. Onboarding in days, not months — and the rituals that made it work.

Talent xR&D Talent · Da Nang
15 Mar 2026
Issue 001

In late 2025, a Tokyo-based IT services firm asked us if we could place five AI-native engineers into one of their existing client teams. The team needed to ship a new product. They had Java engineers, they had project managers, they did not have anyone who could write a prompt without breaking it. We placed five graduates of the pipeline. They were billing inside a week. This is the build log.

01 The askWhat the client needed.

The IT services firm had won a build engagement with a JP enterprise that included an AI feature set they couldn't staff internally. Conventional placement would have meant a six-month hiring cycle. They came to us asking for a temporary embed — five engineers for nine months — to ship the feature and quietly upskill the firm's existing team.

  • Headcount — five engineers, full-time embedded
  • Duration — nine months, with a three-month renewal option
  • Brief — ship the AI feature set + transfer practice to the firm's senior engineers
  • Conditions — JP-language fluency, JP working hours, JP client conduct

02 OnboardingThe first week.

Conventional onboarding for a placed engineer at a JP client is six to eight weeks — security paperwork, codebase orientation, team introductions, slow ramp into real work. Our pipeline graduates shipped their first PR on day three. The acceleration came from preparation, not corner-cutting.

Three weeks before the start date, the placement team ran the engineers through the firm's actual codebase, mocked the firm's actual ticketing flow, and ran the firm's actual security review with the firm's actual reviewers. By the time the engineers showed up at the Tokyo office, none of the standard friction points were unfamiliar. They were ready to work.

03 The ritualsThree things that worked.

Daily JP standup. The placed engineers ran a 15-minute daily standup with the firm's existing team in Japanese. Not as a language exercise — as integration. By week two they were the team's first port of call for AI-related questions, which is exactly the dynamic we wanted to set up.

Weekly review back to Da Nang. Friday afternoon, the placed engineers ran a 30-minute review back to the Da Nang studio. Tokyo joined for the first ten minutes; the back half was internal. This kept the engineers connected to the pipeline and gave us a feedback loop on the placement experience.

Pair-programming as a default. Each placed engineer paired with a firm engineer for at least two hours a day. The firm's engineers picked up the AI-native workflow by osmosis. No formal training; pair time only.

The firm's senior engineers were sceptical for the first three weeks and converts by week six. Pair-programming did the work. We barely had to argue for the workflow.
Placement lead · Talent

04 What surprised usTwo things.

Surprise one: the firm's existing engineers absorbed the AI workflow faster than we'd budgeted for. Six months in, three of the firm's senior engineers were running their own AI-native review rituals without our engineers in the room. The placement turned into a quiet upskilling engagement on top of the build.

Surprise two: the engineers wanted to stay longer. After the initial nine months, four of the five extended for the renewal option. One transferred to the firm permanently — which is fine; the placement model anticipates this and the engineer remains in the xR&D extended network.

05 What we'd refineFor the next placement.

Two refinements for the next engagement:

  • Earlier ramp-down on day-to-day delivery — the placed engineers absorbed too much of the work in months 2–4. We'll structure the next placement so 30% of their time is explicit upskilling rather than delivery, even if it slows the build slightly.
  • Formal weekly office-hours for firm engineers — informal pair time worked, but a scheduled hour each week for non-pair questions would have caught a wider set of upskilling opportunities.

06 What's nextThe third cohort.

The third cohort of pipeline graduates includes seven engineers slated for placement engagements. Two are bound for the same firm's second project. Three are heading to a different IT services firm we've started working with. Two are going onto a direct-client engagement with no intermediary.

The placement model has stabilised. We'll publish the longer-form playbook this autumn. Briefly: prepare three weeks ahead, run JP standups daily, pair as default, weekly review home. The rest is engineer quality, which the pipeline already delivers.
