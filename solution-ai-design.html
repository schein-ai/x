In brief

A generative studio partner that widens — not replaces — the design space.

From the first napkin sketch, our generative co-pilots test thousands of massings, façades, and circulation patterns against site, brief, and program in minutes, not months. The studio reviews, edits, and ships — every output is a starting point your designers can take further.

Built for architectural practices that already have strong design culture and want to amplify it. Not a generic SaaS, not a black box: we tune the model on your studio's reference library, your typological preferences, your local code envelope, and the constraints you actually care about.

FAQ

Common questions.

The questions principals ask in the first conversation. Don't see yours? Email a partner.

    Will the co-pilot replace our junior designers?

    No, and we wouldn't sell it as one. The co-pilot widens the design space; juniors and seniors curate it, draw the chosen scheme into design development, and detail it. Studios that have run the practice for a year report more hours spent on craft per project, not fewer — they just spend less time chasing dead ends.

    What happens to our IP and our reference library?

    It stays yours. The fine-tuning happens on infrastructure you control (or xR&D-managed with strict tenancy). Your weights, your data, your audit logs. The model never trains on a tenant other than yours, and it is never used to train base models elsewhere.

    Enterprise tier ships with full source-available implementation, so your security team can audit every line.

    What software do we need?

    Rhino 7 or 8 with Grasshopper is the minimum. Revit 2024+ for BIM export. Browser for the web co-pilot UI. We provide a Grasshopper plugin and a Revit add-in; both are signed and run inside your existing licences. No SaaS lock-in.

    Can we start without committing to a retainer?

    Yes — that's the Pilot tier. A single 6-week sprint on a real project. Most practices use the pilot to evaluate fit; about 78% convert to a Studio retainer afterward, but there's no obligation either way and you keep all output regardless.

    How does this fit alongside your other practices?

    AI-Enhanced Design (this practice) shapes early concept. Data-Informed Architecture compiles the site dossier that feeds the co-pilot. Performance Optimization takes a chosen scheme into deeper simulation. Smart Delivery manages BIM and construction. They share data layers but ship as separate engagements.
