Featured paper · April 2026
Track 01 · Climate-AI · Pre-print

Generative massing under monsoon: a reinforcement-learning framework for tropical resilience.

doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.71428
187Citations
14Co-runs
2.4kDownloads
Abstract

Coastal cities in the lower Mekong face a coupled problem: peak-monsoon flooding events have grown 36% more frequent since 2010, while building stock is the principal driver of regional embodied emissions. This paper proposes a reinforcement-learning framework that drafts early-stage building massings against a 35-year synthetic monsoon record, optimising jointly for flood-line clearance, cross-ventilation, and embodied carbon.

We test the framework on a 76,500 m² civic infrastructure brief in Cần Thơ, Vietnam, and report a 34% reduction in embodied carbon against the regional baseline, alongside a 41× improvement in stormwater retention compared to a hardened-edge alternative. We discuss limitations of the synthetic record under non-stationary climate, and release the agent's training pipeline under MIT licence at github.com/xrd-studio/monsoon-rl.

How we work

Studio-grade, peer-reviewed.

Every research thread cycles through the same four-phase loop. We name the phases plainly because that's what the calendar looks like. Each phase has its own toolset and its own critical reader.

01

Open the question

A field workshop with academic and civic partners. We define the question, the critical reader, and what "done" looks like — a paper, a tool, a building, or all three.

Field workshopLiving brief
02

Build the instrument

Datasets compiled, simulation harnesses written, sensors specified. Every model is paired with a 1:1 prototype on a real site within six months of kick-off.

PythonRhinoOpenFOAMEnergyPlus
03

Run & measure

Field deployment, post-occupancy sensing, A/B against the baseline. Every dataset is preregistered; every result we publish has the negative runs on the same drive.

SensorsPOE protocolsOSF preregistration
04

Publish & ship

Pre-print first, peer review next, open code at submission. Every method we publish ships back into our project pipeline within a year — or it gets archived.
