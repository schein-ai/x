The premise

A roadmap, not a report.

Big AI strategy reports tend to weigh a lot and move very little. The client had read those reports before, from name-brand consultancies, and were tired of the format. What they wanted was the question after the report — which pilot to fund first, why, and on what timeline — answered up front.

We worked function by function across the firm and the five ASEAN sites. Each function got the same five questions; each candidate AI investment got the same scoring. By the end, the deck had thinned out and the queue had filled in.

The engagement closed with three PoC plans ready for kickoff and a Year-1 sequencing for the rest. ① engineering started on the first PoC the next week.

The approach

Four moves, kickoff-ready output.

The assessment is structured to end with a queue, not a list. Each move sets up actionable work for the team that's going to build it.

Move 01

Function decomposition

Map the business by function across all sites. Each function gets the same five questions, the same data structure, the same scoring rubric.

Move 02

Candidate identification

For each function, identify AI investment candidates. Concrete enough to scope; abstract enough to compare.

Move 03

Prioritise & sequence

Score every candidate (impact, effort, risk, dependency). Sequence into Year-1 / Year-2. Build the PoC queue.

Move 04

Hand off to build

Top three PoCs get full plans: scope, success criteria, team, timeline. ① engineering picks up the next week.
